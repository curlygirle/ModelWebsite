How does UI relate to interactivity: When we look at what UI and interactivity are they are defined as follows: UI is the graphical representation of a program or system that allows users to interact with it and Interactivity is how the user can interact with the UI and the system that it represents and to what degree. Therefore, a well-designed UI should be easy and fast, allowing users a full understanding of the features provided along with how to interact with them. The level of interactivity in the UI could have a major influence on the user experience, influencing how engaging and satisfying the interaction is for the user. An example of this is clear and easy-to-use navigation menus, clickable buttons, sliders, dropdown menus etc. which can help users to engage more actively with the website and achieve their outcomes more efficiently.

Reflection on Reading: When we look at the article on ‘User Experience – A Research Agenda’ by Hassenzahl and Tractinsky dives into the concept of user experience and discusses a better method when trying to understand and define user experience. One of the writers' main issues is the requirement for a precise definition of UX. They contend that, while user experience (UX) is a frequently used term in the field of human-computer interaction (HCI) and design, there is still no agreement on its meaning.


With this the reading shows us why there is a need for us to explore the various dimension of user experience. Overall, the essay gives useful insights into the idea of user experience and recommends a study agenda to expand our understanding of this topic. It highlights the need of having a clear concept of UX, exploring multiple elements of UX, taking context into account, and using qualitative approaches in UX research.


Reference: Hassenzahl, M. & Tractinsky, N., 2006. User experience-a research agenda. Behaviour & information technology, 25(2), pp.91-97.
